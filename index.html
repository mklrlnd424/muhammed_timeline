Muhammad Ali: A Brief History

Muhammad Ali, the greatest boxer to ever step into the ring.

1942: Born Cassius Marcellus Clay, Jr., on Jan. 17, in Louisville, Ky., to Odessa and Cassius, Sr.

1954: Following the theft of his bike, Clay began training under Fred Stoner and over the next 6 years went on to win 6 Kentucky Golden Glove championships, two national Golden Glove titles, and two Amateur Athletic Union crowns.

1960: Clay wins the light-heavyweight gold medal at the Summer Olympics in Rome with a unanimous decision over Poland's Zbigniew Pietrzykowski

1964: Clay has first bout with Sonny Liston. Liston refuses to leave his corner after the 6th round and Clay becomes the heavyweight champion of the world. Clay also announces he has become a Black Muslim and changes his name to Muhammad Ali.

1965: Clay defeats Sonny Liston again to retain his heavyweight title. The image above was taken after Clay, now Ali, drops Liston with the "phantom punch"

1967: Ali refuses induction into the U.S. Army do to his religious and moral stance on the Vietnam war stating "I ain't got no quarrel with those Vietcong" and is stripped of his WBA titles and license to fight. In addition, the court finds him guilty of draft evasion fining him $10000, and sentencing him to 5 years of prison. Ali remains free, pending multiple appeals.

1970: Finding a loophole with Georgia having no state boxing commission, Ali fights Jerry Quarry in Atlanta, knocking him out in three rounds

1971: Ali fights heavyweight champ Joe Frazier in Madison Square Garden. Ali is dropped with a left hook in the 15th round and ends up losing by unanimous decision. Three months later, the Supreme Court overturns the draft evasion conviction.

1974: Ali and Frasier go at it again and Ali wins the bout. On Oct. 30th the famed "Rumble in the Jungle" event takes place between Ali and George Foreman. The event results in Foreman being knocked out in the eighth round.

1978: Ali loses to Leon Spinks in the 1976 Olympics in a 15 round split decision. Ali rematches Spinks 7 months later to regain the title with a unanimous decision victory.

1979: Ali announces his retirement on June 27th.

1980: Ali comes out of retirement to fight the current heavyweight champ Larry Holmes. Holmes defeats Ali decidedly, knocking him out in the 11th round.

1984: Ali is diagnosed with Parkinson's disease.

1996: Ali carries the Olympic torch and ignites the cauldron to signal the beginning of the Summer Olympics in Atlanta.

2016: Ali was hospitalized with pneumonia on June 2, 2016. His conditioned worsened over the next day and he died the following day after going in to septic shock.
